Resources, Tools and Software

Microsoft Teams

Microsoft Teams will be the primary source of communication between the group members as well as between group members and their course mentor and lecturer who will be keeping progress of the team. Teams meetings will occur at least once a week and web cams will be switched on. Meetings will also be recorded for future reference.

Canvas

Canvas will be our main source for resources and updates regarding where to procede with our assignment, which can/will include announcements from our tutor or Dr. B, Assignment specifications and weekly resources in the modules to help us get an idea on where our assignment should be at during the designated week.


Trello

Much like Assignment 1, we will be using Trello to outline what tasks will need to be completed and the timeframe we will need to complete them in. Specifically for this assignment, we will be having a dedicated Trello card for MVF, and having information and diagrams within each one. Trello will also be where we post information of our contributions for each week for our weekly standups, allowing us to easily present our information along with substantial evidence for each contribution.


Figma

A new tool we had not used previously in Assignment 1 was Figma, which we used to construct and create our prototype/concept designs for our mobile application of Quick Parking. This was mostly handled by Daniel, but was made accessible to all of us including those who wish to view and test out the prototype. Some of us had previously used this tall in User-Centered Design, as such we felt it was appropriate to use as we were familiar with how it functioned.


GitHub

GitHub will be the main hosting source for uploading our files and media for our website. Much like in assignment 1, we will be using this as a collaborative workspace to create and and share our html files, as well as edit them and add information on to them. This includes information from our Trello cards and any other outstanding documents.
