USER STORIES

  • Researching about the type of users that would like likely be using the application.
  • Talking to potential clients and analysing their needs and desires of the ideal application.
  • Analysing how the current application and MVF's will be able to cater the needs of the user.
  • Designing the layout of the user stories and aligning it to the existing features.

ARCHITECTURE DIAGRAM

  • Researching about architecture diagrams and how to construct one.
  • Analysing the types of information that is required to be added into the diagram.
  • Provide justification and explanation of the architectural design and how it is beneficial for the user.

VALIDATION CRITERIA

  • Research about the structure of the validation criteria.
  • Compare the MVF's to what is expected by the user and how the expectations will be met.

LUCID TEXT DESCRIPTION OF QUICK BOOKING MINIMUM VIABLE FEATURE

MVF1 - 25 hours

Five hours + Five hours + Five hours + Seven hours + Three hours = Twenty-five hours

This will likely be one of the largest sections of our app as it requires working knowledge of data storage so that we can securely store our users private information, as well as devising an interface that is easy to interact with. The load can easily be split six ways so that we all do a fair amount of work and still hit a fair target for the estimated time.


MVF2 - 12 hours

Two hours + Two hours + Two hours + Four hours + Two hours = Twelve hours

Given that MVF1 will give us the basic layout of the app it's a matter of replicating this interface and using it to device the quick bookings section. The knowledge of data storage from MVF1 will mean that we will already know how to store this booking data so it is accessible later by the user.


MVF3 - 20 hours

Two hours + Four hours + Four hours + Six hours + Four hours = Twenty hours

This will likely be the most time-consuming part of the project as it requires coding so that the users phone GPS can be securely accessed so that QuickPark can guide them to the parking garage. For assignment purposes, this might be left out but still alluded to (or a dummy procedure implemented) as it will likely take a long time to get this done and won't fit within the time constraints of the project.

MVF4 - 15 hours

Two hours + Two hours + Three hours + Six hours + Two hours = Fifteen hours

15 hours should be sufficient enough to get this part done. Two hours of learning how to handle payment data. One hour of research for how to implement this. One hour of design, as we already have prototypes to base it off and the other MVF's. Seven hours coding and then four hours testing (over a course of time not one block of seven hours).


MVF5 - 12 hours

One hour + One hour + Four hours + Four hours + Two hours = Twelve hours

Twelve hours should be sufficient. Contact Us is far simpler compared to other features and would require a text box and form which will be emailed to a designated email address. Learning at most will be one hour. Research three hours. Design is two hours. Coding is four hours and two hours for testing.

EVF1 - 25 hours

Two hours + Two hours + Eight hours + Ten hours + Three hours = Twenty-five hours

This will take some time as it will require making amendments to the current registration system so it can fit business needs as well as individuals. A new storage solution will be needed to securely hold business users' data as well as their booking history and receipts.


EVF2 - 20 hours

Two hours + Four hours + Eight hours + Four hours + Two hours = Twenty hours

This will take time not just for coding but also for research as it requires us to learn the security and privacy protocols of both Apple and Google and get approval to implement our app into it so that users can access QuickPark using Apple and Google Maps. The design will be similar to the design of the Live Tracking & GPS built into our app

EVF3 - 20 hours

One hour + Five hours + Four hours + Eight hours + Two hours = Twenty hours

This will require a lot of research and putting in new data into the app that can be used to compare prices. Mainly the parking prices of each parking garage which the final cost is generated from when a user inputs the date and time that they'd like to book. The app would then need to order these by lowest-to-highest
