MVF1: Registration and Profile Editing : 15 hours
  • 2 hours: learning
  • 2 hours: research
  • 3 hours: design
  • 6 hours: coding
  • 2 hours: testing

Registration and Profile editing will be the first feature that user encounters. Extensive research should be conducted by browsing through other applications and taking inspiration from their registration processes, which can account for the 2 hours of research and learning. Design is important for any section of an application, especially if this page will determine whether the user ends up registering an account and use the application, 3 hours of design should be adequate for this. Extensive knowledge of both local and cloud data storage will be required for this, so 6 hours of coding should be the bare minimum in ensuring that this feature runs as intended, ranging from validation and storage of user account information. This feature is perhaps the most important one to be working properly, so it should be extensively tested for every possible user scenario to ensure that a user won't experience any issues, 2 hours of testing should be fine for this.


MVF2: Quick Booking : 20 hours
  • 4 hours: learning
  • 3 hours: research
  • 3 hours: design
  • 7 hours: coding
  • 3 hours: testing

The quick bookings feature is the main feature of the application, being able to book a parking spot through the application with minimal effort. This feature will need extensive communication with servers in order to process booking requests and ensure that a booking is valid and available. Browsing through similar applications and noting similarities would be a good place to start, and knowledge of reading and writing to data storage locations will be required, so the combined 7 hours of learning and research would be appropriate for this situation. Once again, the design of this feature's interface would mean the difference between a happy user and an unhappy user, meaning the difference between a user who successfully makes a booking and one who has no luck at all. 3 hours of design work should be adequate for this feature. 7 hours of coding would be the best-case scenario for this feature, assuming there are no issues with connecting with the data server to get information about bookings as well as uploading information about the current booking being completed. This feature must work perfectly, as if it doesn't, could mean even more issues with other aspects of the application. 3 hours of testing should be adequate for this, testing the different possible scenarios and ensuring there is extensive error prevention.


MVF3: Live Tracking and Built-in GPS : 15 hours
  • 2 hours: learning
  • 4 hours: research
  • 3 hours: design
  • 5 hours: coding
  • 1 hours: testing

The live tracking and GPS feature is one which wouldn't be too difficult to implement. One could utilise the built-in map application on the user's device by simply launching, or linking it, from with the Quick Park application. 2 hours of learning and 4 hours of research should be allocated to looking into the map API, such as Apple Maps and Google Maps, and how these could be integrated within the application. 3 hours of design should be allocated to this feature, ensuring that the map and navigation display correctly, and would be viewable and varied screen sizes. 1 hour of testing should be suitable for this feature by testing several addresses of parking locations and confirming that the directions and the map are displayed correctly.


MVF4: Payment: 10 hours
  • 2 hours: learning
  • 2 hours: research
  • 2 hours: design
  • 3 hours: coding
  • 1 hours: testing

The payment feature would also not be too difficult to implement into the application. One could utilise in built in payment system on the user's device, Apple Pay, etc. 2 hours of learning and 2 hours of research should be allocated on looking into how to manage payment information, ensuring high security and privacy, and how one could use online payment systems such as PayPal. 2 hours of design would be suitable for designing an appealing payment interface so that the user is always exposed to the current status of the system. 3 hours of coding would be suitable in ensuring proper error prevention and that all validation has been applied to fields.


MVF5: Contact Us and Feedback: 7 hours
  • 1 hours: learning
  • 1 hours: research

  • 2 hours: coding
  • 0.5 hours: testing

The contact and feedback page would be the simplest feature to develop, as all that is required are two forms, and the ability to send those forms to an email, such as the quick park admin. 2 hours of learning and research should be allocated to this task, to browse through possible design ideas of other forms, as well as how to submit a form to an email address. 2.5 hours should be suitable for designing all forms and making sure they are appealing to the user. 2 hours for coding and ensuring validation has been implemented. Half an hour for testing, as all you need to test is that the form has been submitted and received by the quick park admin email.
